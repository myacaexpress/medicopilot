Why PECL + MSP moved up
Compliance is the single highest-stakes surface in Medicare sales. Before: PECL lived in a muted footer on the Call Info tab — easy to miss. After: a dedicated Compliance Hub sits at the top of the copilot card's left column with an amber-tinted gradient, progress gauge, and MSP broken out as a pulsing action-required card. The agent cannot start selling plans without it in their peripheral vision.
New compliance affordances
Compliance score (3/5) with gauge · color-coded risk dots (red = required, amber = recommended, green = done) · required/rec tags per row · MSP card with one-click "Insert script" button that pushes the disclosure into the active AI response · plan cards carry a loud solid-amber MSP badge until covered, then flip solid-green.
Trade-off called out
The Compliance Hub eats ~180px of vertical space on the left column. That's deliberate: if compliance doesn't feel heavy, it won't get respected. In float-only mode (no split), the hub collapses to a single strip showing only the gauge + "⚠ 2 required" — tap-to-expand returns the full hub.
